04

Connecticut · 8-30g Board

Gold Coast + Stamford/New Haven — real CT CAMA data with zoning-override flagging
The play: in towns under 10% affordable (Greenwich 5.3%, Westport 3.5%, Ridgefield, New Canaan, Darien), CGS 8-30g lets a 30%-affordable set-aside override local zoning denial — so estate-zoned vacant land becomes developable. Unit counts assume ~16/acre WHERE 8-30g applies (~8/acre elsewhere) — verify with an affordability plan. Stamford/New Haven are 8-30g-exempt (over threshold). Screen tier: confirm zoning, wetlands, and sewer before pursuing.

05

New Jersey · Redevelopment Board

Bergen/Hudson Gold Coast + GWB corridor — real MOD-IV parcel data
The play: NJ's lever is the 40A:20 long-term tax exemption PILOT — once a site is in a municipal "Area in Need of Redevelopment," the town can convey it directly (no bid) with a ~30-year PILOT (~10-15% of revenue vs. full taxes). This board leads with vacant + commercial/industrial (class 4A/4B) parcels — the ones that become redevelopment sites. Screen tier: assessed values are NOT market (apply town ratio); verify redevelopment designation on the municipal map. Owner data is sparse in the NJ layer.
